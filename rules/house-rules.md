## Conventions

- **Always round up.** Any fraction rounds up, always. This *is* official DC20, but in 0.10.5 the rulebook only states it **inside an example**, not as a standalone rule (the explicit statement looks to have been dropped between versions), so we surface it here explicitly. Kind-of-official, kind-of-house.

## Sense Magic (Spellblade): as played

**RAW** (`classes.md`): *Sense Magic* is a flavour feature: 1 minute focusing to detect specific **creature types** (Aberration, Celestial, Elemental, Fey, Fiend, Undead) within 20 Spaces, via a Spell Check vs each creature's Mental Save.

**As played at this table (broader):** it senses **magic generally**: not just the listed creature types, but the magic such creatures exude, *and* the magic of other creatures, items, and **effects**. Adjudication is loose and GM-driven, and has sometimes been called as an **Awareness** check rather than a Spell Check (a nat-20 can surface magic on NPCs, items, or a concealed presence). Treat this broader reading as canon; don't "correct" it back to RAW when giving mechanical help. Flag RAW only if asked for the book version.

## Design-math rules of thumb (from the DC20 devs, relayed via Darryl)

From the devs' behind-the-scenes design-math notes (shared informally / in sneak-peeks, relayed via Darryl; **not printed in the rulebook**, but the designers' own numbers, not a table invention). Use them to weight resource-spend advice and encounter budgeting:

- **1 MP = 2 SP = 2 AP.** SP is at AP parity but, for classes that regenerate it (e.g. Spellblade, see the errata in `_INDEX.md`), effectively renewable.
- **~6 rounds/day** (2 Combats × 3 rounds): the assumed combat day.
- **1 Talent = 1 Class Feature = 4 Ancestry Points = 8 AP/day**: the value of a build pick.
- **~65% to-hit between equal-level combatants**: the assumed accuracy baseline for an equal-level monster vs a PC (and the reverse). Use it for any to-hit / expected-damage / defence comparison (e.g. a +5 attack hitting on 8+, Heavy on 13+, crit on 20). *(The related point that one DisADV is cancellable 1-for-1 by an attacker spending 1 AP on ADV is RAW, not part of this heuristic.)*

Worked example (Tanrielle, L4): 6 MP ÷ ~6 rounds ≈ **1 MP/round** sustainable, so her Mana Spend Limit of 2 MP/action is a **nova/peak spend** (a fight-opener to swing the balance early, a boss fight, or a "things went wrong" button), not an every-round default. This is the lens for "is this spend sustainable?" in build and play advice.

## Free rebuild on ruleset updates

Every time DC20 ships a new ruleset version, **all PCs get a free full rebuild** to realign with the new rules (options change; some cease to exist entirely). Convention: keep the character's *identity* stable, making the **minimum** theme/mechanical tweaks needed to function and to explore newly-available options; avoid large reinventions. **Maneuvers and spells change most often** in these rebuilds. Done at the 0.10.5 release; expect another at **0.11**. *(Relevance now: this is the first time Tanrielle has been played since the 0.10.5 rebuild, so small maneuver/spell tweaks are legitimately within the rebuild window, good timing for the enabling adjustments under discussion.)*
