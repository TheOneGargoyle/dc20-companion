Build or level your character, yourselfplayer how-to

The party's character builder is a web page. It runs the campaign's full rules engine in your browser, checks every pick live, and nothing you do can break the party sheet. No install, no account, no git.

1Open the builder

theonegargoyle.github.io/dc20-companion/builder.html

Bookmark it. The first load takes a little while (it pulls the rules engine, a few MB); after that it's quick. Jump straight to your character with ?char=runt on the end (handles: tanrielle, runt, minimus, bonan, scaletrix, xanwyn), or start a brand-new character with ?new=druid (spellblade, warlock, commander, barbarian, druid). The dropdown at the top does the same thing.

2Make your changes

Pick options from the dropdowns, use Add level on level-up night, or respec anything. Every edit is re-checked instantly against the real rules:

  • green all checks passed: the build is legal.
  • red something's illegal (over-spent points, off-list pick). Fix it before you send.
  • amber advisory only: you have unspent points left. Legal, but check you didn't forget a pick.

Close the tab mid-build? Fine. Your work-in-progress is kept in your browser, and the page offers to resume it next visit.

3Export and send it to Darryl

Two export buttons at the bottom:

  • Respec export (yourname.respec.yaml): what-ifs and experiments. Never touches the real party.
  • CANON export (yourname.yaml): the real deal, for a change we've agreed at the table (like a level-up).

Then send Darryl the downloaded file, or open "view / copy the exported YAML" and paste the text into Discord. That's it, you're done.

4Darryl commits, the party page updates

Darryl re-validates whatever you send (a bad file gets caught, not committed) and lands it in the campaign repo. The Companion rebuilds itself automatically: within a couple of minutes your new numbers are live on the party tab, and the About tab's build stamp shows when it last rebuilt.

Want to keep tinkering later, or on another device? Keep your downloaded YAML and load it back in with the "load a YAML" control. The engine re-checks anything you load.
